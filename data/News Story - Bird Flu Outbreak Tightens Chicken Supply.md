# Bird Flu Outbreak Tightens Regional Chicken Supply, Raising Input Costs

**Date:** 2026-02-24  
**Type:** Supply Chain / Commodity Pressure  
**Region:** Multi-state supplier network affecting Store-001

State agriculture officials confirmed a **bird flu outbreak** at several commercial farms in the broader supply region. While no local food safety restrictions are in place for prepared products, upstream distributors have announced tighter allocation and temporary pricing increases.

## Expected Business Impact
- **Higher wholesale chicken costs** over the next several days
- Increased risk of **stock volatility** and delayed deliveries
- Pressure to improve sell-through and reduce avoidable waste

## Suggested Operational Response
- Increase ordering discipline using recent hourly sell-through trends
- Prioritize cooking windows with strongest historical conversion (lunch/dinner peaks)
- Reduce low-efficiency late-hour batches unless demand signals justify them

## Pricing/Cost Signal for Analysis
Model scenarios with elevated purchase cost (above the baseline `$2/chicken`) to test profit sensitivity and identify break-even adjustments in order volume and cooking cadence.
