# Midday Storm Disrupts Chicken Buying Patterns Across East Side

**Date:** 2026-02-22  
**Type:** Weather / Local Demand Shock  
**Region:** East Side (Store-001 market)

A fast-moving storm cell hit the area between **11:30 AM and 2:30 PM**, bringing heavy rain, lightning, and short-lived street flooding near major intersections. Lunch-hour foot traffic dropped sharply as many customers delayed trips or stayed inside.

## Expected Business Impact
- **Short-term sales dip during peak lunch window** (normally high-volume hours)
- Potential **late-afternoon catch-up demand** once weather clears
- Elevated risk of **midday overproduction waste** if cooking plans are not adjusted quickly

## Suggested Operational Response
- Reduce batch sizes during active storm alerts
- Shift some planned lunch cooks into flexible afternoon replenishment
- Use hourly weather triggers to pause or slow cooking cadence

## Pricing/Cost Signal for Analysis
Weather-driven demand dips can increase unsold units per cooked batch, indirectly raising effective cost per sold chicken for that day.
