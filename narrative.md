# Specification Document (Narrative)

## Story Arc - VIP Customer Lifecycle Management (Expanded)

### Zava Sports – Sales Operations / Customer Lifecycle Intelligence

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The Microsoft team partnered with **Zava’s Director of Sales Operations** to understand key challenges around managing high‑value customer relationships. Zava lacks structured, timely visibility into changes in customer engagement—particularly among VIP and Gold-tier customers who drive the majority of revenue.

To address this, we propose a three‑phase solution using **Copilot Studio**, **Microsoft Foundry**, and **Agent Framework**. Each phase demonstrates how AI can meaningfully assist business users and improve customer retention effectiveness.

This specification captures Zava’s business requirements in business language and defines the expected outputs—the **three demo experiences**—that will later be generated using **GitHub Spec‑Kit**. Students will receive this spec, the original Excel data, and the generated demos in class.

---

## 2. Business Backstory & SME Insights

This solution was shaped by interviews with Zava’s **Director of Sales Operations**, who shared the following insights:

> **“Our top customers often slow down long before they churn. We have no consistent way to detect this early.”**
> **“Order frequency and spend patterns change subtly. By the time we notice, it’s too late to intervene.”**
> **“We don’t need complex forecasting—just practical visibility and timely action.”**
> **“If AI can surface who needs attention and why, our teams can focus on the right relationships.”**

These insights define the business problems that the system—and the student demos—must address.

---

## 3. Business Problem Summary

Zava’s current processes lack a systematic way to track customer lifecycle signals. Key challenges include:

- VIP and Gold-tier customers may quietly reduce purchasing activity.
- The business lacks explanations for customer behavior changes.
- Sales teams receive no recommendations on next steps.
- There is no consistent “customer health model.”
- No automation exists to monitor customer behavior over time.

These problems will be addressed through three progressive phases:

1. **Copilot Studio** – Insight, interpretation, and explanation
2. **Foundry** – Structured transformation and scoring
3. **Agent Framework** – Automated monitoring and alerting

---

## 4. Scope & Phasing

### Phase 1 — Copilot Studio

**Objective:** Identify and explain at-risk customer behavior using conversational AI.
**Student timebox:** ≤ 30 minutes
**Focus:** Insight, reasoning, business value

### Phase 2 — Microsoft Foundry

**Objective:** Transform data to compute Recency, Frequency, Monetary, and customer tiers.
**Student timebox:** ≤ 60 minutes
**Focus:** Data shaping, pipelines, customer health scoring

### Phase 3 — Agent Framework  

**Objective:** Introduce light automation for proactive customer alerts.
**Student timebox:** ≤ 10 minutes
**Focus:** “Explain-only” automation preview

---

## 5. Functional Requirements (Business Language)

### 5.1 Customer Health Identification

The system must enable Zava to identify customers exhibiting:

- Slowing order frequency
- Large gaps between orders
- Declining spend
- High-risk behavior among VIP/Gold tiers

**Outcome:** Identify “who needs attention right now.”

---

### 5.2 Customer Health Explanation

The system must provide explanations, including:

- Recency changes
- Frequency drops
- Spend/monetary changes
- Margin anomalies
- Product mix shifts

**Outcome:** Understand *why* a customer is at risk.

---

### 5.3 Recommended Human Actions  

The system must recommend:

- Retention outreach
- Account owner follow-ups
- Pricing review for low-margin VIP orders
- Targeted offer guidance

**Outcome:** Clear next steps for Zava teams.

---

### 5.4 Customer Portfolio Summary  

The system must summarize:

- Tier distributions (VIP, Gold, Silver, Bronze)
- At-risk concentration
- Emerging trends across the customer base

**Outcome:** Leadership-level situational awareness.

---

### 5.5 Support for Specification-Driven Development

This specification must guide **GitHub Spec‑Kit** to generate:

- Demo steps for Copilot Studio
- Derived-data instructions and Foundry pipelines
- Agent Framework sample logic
- All within the time and student-level constraints

## 6. Data Requirements

Zava provides one source file:
**Zava Sales Data – FY2024–2026.xlsx**

The system must support:

- Reading customer-level and order-level data
- Deriving Recency, Frequency, Monetary
- Creating tiering heuristics

### Derived Data Requirements

If additional synthesized or derived fields are required:

- **The demo steps generated by Spec‑Kit must include instructions to compute or generate them from the Excel file.**

---

## 7. Non-Functional Requirements

- Outputs should be understandable by non-technical business leaders.
- Exercises must be Level 300 with optional Level 400 complexity.
- Demo time constraints:
  - Copilot Studio ≤ 30 minutes
  - Foundry ≤ 60 minutes
  - Agent Framework ≤ 10 minutes
- Design should enable future extensibility.

---

## 8. Requirements for Demo Generation (for GitHub Spec‑Kit)

### 8.1 Copilot Studio Demo

Must:

- Use conversational interactions, not topic construction
- Demonstrate:
  - At-risk identification
  - Customer health explanation
  - Recommended actions

- Include Level 400 optional extensions

---

### 8.2 Microsoft Foundry Demo

Must:

- Compute RFM values, tiers, and simple health indicators
- Use the simplest path to derive fields from the Excel file
- Include optional Level 400 extensions such as:
  - Customer Health Index
  - Data visualization
  - Additional segment logic

---

### 8.3 Agent Framework Demo

Must:

- Be “explain-only” with minimal hands-on interaction
- Demonstrate a simple rule (e.g., VIP + high recency → alert)
- Include optional after-class extensions:
  - Multi-signal alerting
  - Escalation workflows  
  - Autonomous task creation

- Customer tier definitions may need adjustment; keep thresholds flexible.  
- Focus on value communication, not technical precision.
